# Trends in Horticulture Crops in India: Spices and Floriculture

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes the trends in **Area, Production, and Yield** of major spice and flower crops grown in India over a 10-year period (2011-12 to 2020-21). The analysis was conducted as part of a research internship at the **Giri Institute of Development Studies (GIDS), Lucknow**, under the guidance of Dr. Nomita P. Kumar.

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze the trend of Area, Production, and Yield of different spices and flowers in India
- Identify emerging patterns and changes in area and production over time
- Analyze changes in yield across different crop categories
- Suggest measures to maximize area and production of horticulture crops

## 📊 Data Source
- Department of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, Government of India
- National Horticulture Board (NHB)
- Time period: 2011-12 to 2020-21 (10 years)

## 🔍 Methodology
Secondary data was compiled and classified into three groups based on production volume:
- **Top produced** crops
- **Middle-range produced** crops
- **Least produced** crops

Yield was calculated using:
Yield = Total Production / Total Area

## 📈 Key Findings

**Spices:**
- Garlic, chillies, and ginger are the top-produced spices in India, followed by turmeric, cumin, coriander, and tamarind
- Cinnamon/Tejpat, clove, and saffron/vanilla are the least-produced spices
- Both area and production show a rising trend over the decade, with a corresponding increase in yield

**Flowers:**
- Rose, gladiolus, tuberose, and chrysanthemum are the top-produced flowers
- Anthurium, orchid, and tulip are the least-produced flowers
- Marigold shows the largest cultivated area among flowers

**Overall:**
- Increase in area under cultivation directly correlates with increase in production and yield
- Farmers are increasingly diversifying from traditional crops to spices and flowers due to better profitability and employment generation
